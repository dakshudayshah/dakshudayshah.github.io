When I've Failed

Quick note: All too often, people highlight their successes and hide their failures. I believe this contributes to impostor syndrome and a toxic culture of one-upmanship. I personally have failed many times at many things. I hope by sharing some of my failures here, I can help change the perception about failure both for myself and others.


Deep Green

In Jun’19, I took the leap to leave the comfort of my job and shifted to America to join my wife and work on my idea for a long distance relationship app full time. It was scary to come to another country with no reputation to back me here. But I persevered, spoke to users and tested multiple hypotheses to build a prototype. However, the startup failed to take off. During this phase, I even got the chance to meet successful entrepreneurs from Y-Combinator and investors in the Bay Area and learn from them. They helped me understand that, perhaps, I had taken on too much without having a personal network in the US. Introspection also highlighted gaps in my technical knowledge and business skills required to run a successful, scalable technology enabled business. As the last 10 months unravelled, I faced many hardships and uncontrollable events - a shortage of relevant skills, visa uncertainty, financial hardships, and now a pandemic. But I haven’t lost my courage. Despite setbacks and false starts, I take each challenge as a learning experience.


Picostone Distributorship

To test my business acumen, I invested my savings to sell Smart Home IoT devices to dealers in satellite cities of Mumbai. While I could leverage my retail business development experience, negotiating and educating dealers for a new product category was painful. I managed to sell my inventory within 2 months while keeping my day job. While I did not make any profits, I got vital lessons in distribution and sales.

Dollar Gujarati

I needed to build my network in America. I also wanted to learn how Immigrant Entrepreneurs had made it in this land of opportunity. So with my wife, Jaynee Jhaveri, and my friend Atit Kothari, I launched a podcast to interview successful Immigrant entrepreneurs and professionals in America. We talk with them about their ideas and stories in America, and hope to learn from the struggles they faced. Hosting this podcast has improved my communication skills by leaps and bounds, and given me access to the thinking of some of the most successful people in the US.
